The conversation about AI has mostly been about capability: what the models
can do, how fast they improve, where they break. That's the visible layer.
Underneath it is a quieter question that I think matters more over the next
decade — **who owns the stack the intelligence runs on.**

Sovereign AI is the idea that a country (or a bloc, or an organization)
should be able to build, run, and govern AI systems without depending on
infrastructure it doesn't control. Not just the model weights, but the
compute, the data pipelines, the energy, and the legal frameworks around all
of it. When every layer of that stack lives somewhere else, "having AI" turns
out to mean something much weaker than it sounds.

For Europe specifically, this is less about catching up on benchmarks and more
about not outsourcing a foundational capability the way it did with cloud and
search. The hard parts aren't only technical — they're industrial policy,
energy, and the willingness to fund unglamorous infrastructure for years.
